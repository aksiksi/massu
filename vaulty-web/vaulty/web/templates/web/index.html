Vaulty has your back.


Store email content and attachments

Any email you send to your Vaulty address is immediately forwarded to your cloud storage account. Currently, we only support Dropbox, but Google Drive and S3 are coming soon.

Sender whitelisting, by default

You can set a list of whitelisted sender addresses for each Vaulty address. Any email received from an address that is not on the whitelist is rejected.

TLS encryption for all emails

All emails you send to our mail servers are TLS encrypted, provided your mail client supports TLS, of course.

Customize your Vaulty address

With the Premium plan, you get to choose a custom Vaulty address, so you can say goodbye to memorizing a randomly generated email address!

So, how does this all work?


Step 1

You create a Vaulty account and link it to one or more cloud storage services, and optionally configure where and how your email content is stored.

Step 2

We provide you with your own, unique Vaulty email address for each cloud storage service (@vaulty.net).

Step 3

Any email you send to your Vaulty address is streamed directly to the cloud storage service: we never view or store the contents on our side.
